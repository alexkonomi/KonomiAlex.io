About Me

I am an associate professor of the Devison of Statistics and Data Sciences in the Department of Mathematical Sciences at the University of Cincinnati. I earned my doctoral degrees in statistics (2011) from Texas A&M Unversity, where I developed expertise in Bayesian methodology and computation. I received my postdoctoral training at the Pacific Northwest National Laboratory 20011-2014.

My research activities have been in a number of areas of statistical methods including Bayesian modeling methodology, computational statistics, spatial statistics, uncertainty quantification (UQ), Monte Carlo inference, and nonparametric regression. Interdisciplinary application areas have included research in remote sensing, climatology, ecology, engineering, and materials science.
